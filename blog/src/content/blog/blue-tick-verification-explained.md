They mean different things on different platforms — and in many cases, they don't really mean anything at all. A green tick on one site might be issued automatically the moment someone uploads a single selfie. A "verified" badge on another might just mean the user paid an extra fee with no actual checks performed.

## What the Blue Tick Confirms

A Blue Tick on a Bluechips London profile means three things have been independently verified by our team:

1. **The companion's identity is real.** A government-issued photographic identity document — passport, driver's licence, or national ID card — has been submitted, examined, and confirmed as genuine.
2. **The person in the photos is the same person who holds that ID.** A live, dated selfie has been submitted alongside the ID document and matched against both the ID photo and the profile pictures by a human reviewer.
3. **The person is over the age of majority.** The date of birth on the verified ID confirms the companion is 18 or older. Bluechips London does not list anyone under 18 under any circumstances.

That is what the badge confirms. It is a deliberately narrow, specific claim. We don't pretend the badge confirms anything else.

## What the Blue Tick Does Not Confirm

It is just as important to be clear about what verification doesn't tell you.

The Blue Tick **does not** confirm:

- The accuracy of any other information on the profile, such as age (only that the person is over 18), nationality, languages spoken, or services offered. These remain self-declared.
- Health or STD status. Some companions display testing dates on their profiles; those are self-reported and we don't independently verify medical results.
- Conduct, character, or reliability. Verification confirms identity; it doesn't predict behaviour.
- That every photo on the profile is recent. We confirm the person in the photos is the same person being verified, but profile photos may have been taken months or years before the current verification.

A Blue Tick is the floor, not the ceiling. It eliminates the most common form of fraud in this industry — fake or stolen photos — but it doesn't replace your own judgment about whether a particular companion is right for you.

## How the Verification Process Works

When a companion on Bluechips London applies for Blue Tick verification, the process is as follows.

### Step 1: Submission

The companion uploads, through a secure encrypted form:

- A clear photograph of a government-issued ID. Acceptable documents include UK passport, EU passport, foreign passport, UK driving licence, and national ID cards from EU and selected other jurisdictions.
- A live selfie taken at the time of submission. We request that the selfie be taken holding the ID document, with the companion's face clearly visible alongside the ID photo, and that the selfie be unfiltered.

These documents are uploaded directly to our private storage, accessible only to authorised members of the review team. They are never shown publicly, never shared with third parties, and never accessible to clients on the platform.

### Step 2: Manual Review

A member of our verification team — never an automated system — examines the submission. The review checks:

- That the ID is a genuine document and not a digital edit. We look for the security features specific to each document type, the photo quality, the consistency of fonts and printing, and other markers that distinguish a real ID from a forgery.
- That the photo on the ID matches the live selfie. We use facial structure, not styling, to make this judgment. Hair colour, makeup, and weight may have changed; bone structure does not.
- That the live selfie matches the profile photos already uploaded by the companion. This is the step that prevents someone from verifying with their own ID and then uploading photos of a different person.

If everything matches, the Blue Tick is issued. If anything is unclear or inconsistent, we contact the companion for additional information. We do not issue a Blue Tick on the basis of a partial or unclear submission.

### Step 3: Ongoing Validity

A Blue Tick is not a one-time-and-forever badge. We retain the right to ask any verified companion to re-verify if their profile photos change substantially, if their account has been inactive for an extended period, or if a credible report is received that suggests the original verification may no longer be accurate.

## Why We Made This Process Manual

The honest answer is that automated systems aren't yet good enough for this kind of work, particularly in the adult industry.

Off-the-shelf identity verification services — the kind used by banks and crypto exchanges — are designed for a different threat model. They check whether you are who you say you are when opening an account. They are not designed to check whether the person in your account photos is the person who holds your ID. That second check is the one that matters most for our context, and there is no commercial automated system we trust to do it reliably.

So we do it ourselves. It costs more. It takes longer. It is, frankly, the slow and unglamorous part of running a verification programme. But it is the only way we know to issue a badge that means what it says.
